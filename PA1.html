Question 2 Fill in all the missing values in the data set:

  • Note: The strategy is to fill the NAs in steps column with the average of total steps in the interval that this NA corresponds to.
dat %>%
    group_by(interval)%>%
    mutate_at(vars(steps),~replace_na(.,mean(.,na.rm=TRUE)))%>%
    ungroup %>%
    is.na %>%
    sum

# s1 is to drop all the NAs in the original data set, and to calculate the mean and median of total steps;

dataset_type mean median
NA dropped 10766.19 10765.00
NA filled 10766.19 10766.19

As can be told from the tibble above, mean total steps didnt change, while median total steps moved up a little bit.

What is the impact of imputing missing data on the estimates of the total daily number of steps?

We replaced NAs with the average steps of its interval. This strategy wont change the mean of total steps, because every interval has the same weight. If the filled value doesnt change the mean within the interval group, it wont change the overall mean. However, this strategy does increase median steps, and thats because there are more valid values, which causes the median value moving to the right of its original position. As long as the new values interpolated to the left are not all zeros, the median value will go up.
